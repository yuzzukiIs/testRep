Fauna of the Aral Desert
Gyurza

Gyurza occupies a special place among vipers. This is the largest and most dangerous representative of the viper family in the fauna of the former USSR. All members of the family are poisonous, and the gyurza is the most poisonous snake found in Russia. In addition, the gyurza is considered one of the most poisonous snakes in the world.

In Latin, gyurza is called Vipera lebetina, which means "coffin viper." In Uzbekistan, it is called kok-ilan (green snake), in Turkmenistan - at-ilan (horse snake). In other countries, the gyurza is called the eastern or Levant viper. And the very name "gyurza" is Azerbaijani. That is what is now rooted in the scientific literature.

Speckled scorpion

The motley scorpion is a twilight-night predator that preys on spiders and other invertebrates. It uses a sharp spike located at the end of the abdomen and is equipped with poisonous glands.

Like any scorpion, it can sting, but the amount of poison it has is scanty, and because of its modest size, it cannot pierce deeply into human skin. However, the sting can be quite painful, similar to a wasp sting. The maximum that can be is an increase in body temperature. A person stung by a motley scorpion feels pain, itching and burning, and swelling and redness of the skin occur at the site of the bite.

The species is distributed from Syria, Turkey and the south of the European part of Russia to Afghanistan, southern Mongolia and northern China.

Tarantula Wolf Spider

In the name of the spider, the name of the Italian city of Taranto is heard, the inhabitants of which supposedly suffered from spider bites more often than others. There is a legend that doctors of the Middle Ages recommended that patients who wanted to get rid of the consequences of a spider bite should dance the tarantella as long as possible.

Tarantulas are amazing because they have eight eyes, with which spiders can see everything that happens around, the viewing angle is 360 degrees.

Tarantulas live in burrows up to 50 centimeters deep. To catch prey, they do not weave a web, but lie in wait for insects, attack and paralyze with poison, which has a terrible ability to turn the victim into ... a liquid that spiders drink after a successful hunt.

Large saxaul humpback

Humpbacks, or bodushki, are a family of insects from the suborder of the cicadas of the order Homoptera. They differ in a strongly developed pronotum, resembling a hump with various outgrowths.

There are 3200 species in the world.

Homoptera insects with unusual outgrowths on the chest (combs, spikes, hooks) that camouflage them on plants. Medium size, pronotum with process protruding backwards, anterior upper part of head with frontoclypeus protruding cone-shaped downwards. Various outgrowths are most developed in representatives of the family in southern tropical latitudes, and vice versa, processes are less bizarre in humpbacks of temperate latitudes.

Saxaul jay

The saxaul jay, and especially its Ili subspecies, is rightfully listed in the Red Book of the Kazakh SSR, since even in a relatively small area it does not live everywhere. This bird clearly avoids plains, as well as places with dense saxaul forests. More often it can be found among takyrs and sand dunes, the slopes of which are overgrown with rare saxaul bushes.

The specific name of the saxaul jay reflects its close relationship with the saxaul. Only on his trees among the sandy sea of ​​dunes does she arrange her nests. In winter, when the vast expanses of the desert are covered with continuous snow cover, which makes it difficult to obtain food, this tree provides the jay and other wintering birds with a rich harvest in the form of flying seeds, makes it possible to lead a sedentary lifestyle, and not fly away, like migratory birds, to overseas countries. In turn, the jay, feeding on the seeds of saxaul, "let the wind blow" most of them, which means it helps to settle it and fix loose sands.

Sand Efa

The bite of a sand efa is incredibly dangerous, the mortality rate of people from a bite is about 20%. The lethal dose of poison is about 5 mg. This is not the most poisonous snake in the world, however, according to statistics, every seventh person on Earth who died from a snakebite was bitten by a sand epha. This snake is not at all afraid of people and often crawls into houses, outbuildings and cellars in search of food. There are cases when ephs settled in residential buildings, right under the floor. Efas are very dangerous during the mating period.

Sand ephs are very small snakes, the body length of which rarely exceeds 1 meter. On average, efas grow up to 70 centimeters in length.

Pallas Snake

Pallas snake is a species of non-venomous snakes from the family of already-shaped. Previously considered as a subspecies of the four-striped snake - Elaphe quatorlineata sauromates.

The average length of adults is 1.2-1.5 m, large specimens can reach 2 m. The dorsal side of adult snakes is brownish-yellow with rows of longitudinal dark spots. In the middle of each light scale there is a dark spot. The ventral side is lighter, monotonous - from pale yellow to bright yellow and orange.

Common muzzle

A rare, not numerous species, represented by the only isolated population in the region, living in a very limited area.

The species has long been considered polytypic. As a result of recent revisions, most subspecies have been assigned species status. Within the south of Siberia, on the territory of the Republic of Altai and Tyva, there is also a species previously considered as one of the subspecies of the common muzzle.

Inhabits mountain forests, bushes, mountain steppes, subalpine meadows. In the Novosibirsk region, the species is confined only to rocky outcrops on the slopes along the river. Berd, where mountain-steppe vegetation with scree is represented.

Gray monitor lizard

In nature, gray monitor lizards inhabit various types of deserts and semi-deserts, such as sandy, clayey, loess and rocky. These animals are found on coastal cliffs, in tugai, periodically entering the lands cultivated by man.

If the monitor lizard cannot solder, run away or was taken by surprise, then it immediately takes a stance - it opens its mouth wide, sticks out its tongue, hisses loudly and whips its tail in different directions, its body swells and becomes wide and flat.

If you scare this reptile in an open area, then it prepares for defense in seconds, jumps 1 meter in height with its tail and throws itself in the face or chest of a person, and on the belly of large animals, while inflicting severe lacerations on camels, horses and donkeys.

Salpuga

Different peoples give the phalanges a variety of names - for example, "Scorpion of the Wind." This name was given to the phalanx for their run, because they can reach speeds of up to 2 km / h.

Very mobile and almost all of them are nocturnal predators. Phalanxes are carnivorous or omnivorous, feeding on termites, bees, darkling beetles, and other small arthropods, but may also eat larger animals such as lizards.

There is no venom, but their bites can have dire consequences. Large phalanges are quite able to bite through human skin. When bitten, they can cause blood poisoning. If, nevertheless, the phalanx has bitten, immediate first aid is needed: rinse the bite with any antiseptic - peroxide, brilliant green, iodine, even vodka or alcohol. If the wound still begins to fester, you need to take antibiotics.

Black vulture

Very large bird, all brownish-black in color, wedge-shaped tail, blue legs. The head is bluish-gray, without feathers, covered only with sparse down, on the neck there is a lush "frill" of elongated feathers. Weight 7.0-12.5 kg, length 100-110, wing 73.0-85.4, wingspan 250-300 cm.

The black vulture is a rare breeding migrant. Inhabits rocky low mountains and gentle foothills of high mountain ranges at altitudes of 1300-1500 m. In spring, it appears from late February to early April. Breeds in separate pairs at a distance of 200-1500 m from each other, as well as in colonies of 2-4 pairs.

Centipede

Centipedes bite through human skin and secrete a venom that causes pain, burning, erythema, edema, and lymphangitis. Dizziness, nausea, anxiety are possible; rhabdomyolysis and renal failure have been described. In the US, most centipede bites are from the desert centipede, which can grow up to 15 cm in length.

In the wild, the giant centipede is a successful, dexterous and cruel hunter, eating various invertebrates, as well as frogs, birds, mice, including sometimes even bats, and generally anyone it can get to.
